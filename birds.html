Birds

While there is always something interesting to observe, you will find the greatest numbers of birds at the refuge from early November to late January. In the spring and fall, migratory bird species are moving through the refuge, resulting in high numbers of species.

  1. Golden Eagle

    Aquila chysaetos

    2. INFORMATION:

    The golden eagle (Aquila chrysaetos) is a bird of prey that lives in the Northern Hemisphere. It is the most widespread species of eagle. Golden eagles are North America's largest predatory bird. They are dark brown with long, broad wings. Their length ranges from 70 to 84 cm, and their wingspan ranges from 185 to 220 cm. Females are much larger than males.

  2. Burrowing Owl

    Athene cuninularia

    INFORMATION:

    These small, long-legged owls are found in open landscapes across North and South America. They live in areas with low vegetation, such as grasslands, deserts, and agricultural fields. They nest in underground burrows, often in abandoned mammal burrows.

  3. Chestnut-collared Longspur

    Calcarius ornatus

    INFORMATION:

    The chestnut-collared longspur (Calcarius ornatus) is a small, ground-feeding bird that primarily eats seeds. They are a member of the Calcariidae family. They breed in prairie habitats in Canada and the northern United States, and winter in the United States and Mexico.

  4. Semipalmated Sandpiper

    Calidris pusilla

    INFORMATION:

    The semipalmated sandpiper (Calidris pusilla) is a small shorebird. The name "pusilla" is Latin for "very small". The genus name "kalidris" comes from Aristotle's term for some gray-colored waterside birds.

  5. Piping Plover

    Charadrius melodus

    INFORMATION:

    It is a migratory bird that nests and feeds along coastal sand and gravel beaches in North America. The piping plover is about 7.25 inches (18 cm) long and weighs between 1.5 and 2.2 oz (43-63 grams). It has a short, stout bill and very pale upperparts that are the color of dry sand.

  6. Black Tern

    Chlidonias niger

    INFORMATION:

    The Black Tern (Chlidonias niger) is a small, dark tern that lives in or near inland water in Europe and North America. In the breeding season, the Black Tern has a dark gray body with black head and underparts. In the winter, the Black Tern has a whitish-gray body with dark gray wings and rump.

  7. Northern Harrier

    Circus cyaneus

    INFORMATION:

    The hen harrier (Circus cyaneus) is a bird of prey that breeds in Eurasia. The name "hen harrier" comes from its former habit of preying on free-ranging fowl. It migrates to more southerly areas in winter.

  8. Marsh Wren

    Cistothorus palustris

    INFORMATION:

    A small songbird that is native to North America. It is also known as the long-billed marsh wren. Marsh wrens are often heard but not seen. They are known for their reedy, gurgling sounds that can be heard in cattail and bulrush marshes across North America.

  9. Long-Tailed Duck

    Clangula hyemalis

    INFORMATION:

    A medium-sized sea duck that breeds in the Arctic and winters along the northern coastlines of the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. It is the only member of the genus Clangula.

  10. Trumpeter Swan

    Cygnus buccinator

    INFORMATION:

    The largest swan in North America. It is also the largest waterfowl species, with a wingspan of up to 10 ft 2 in. Trumpeter swans are white and have a long neck and large, black bill.
