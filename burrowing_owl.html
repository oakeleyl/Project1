Burrowing Owl

Burrowing owls are active during the day and night. They hunt for insects and small mammals, including mice, gophers, beetles, earwigs, and grasshoppers. They are important to humans and the ecosystem because they consume agricultural pests and help prevent the spread of diseases. The burrowing owl's scientific name comes from Athena, the Greek goddess of wisdom, and the Latin word cunicularia, which means a miner or burrower. The burrowing owl's population has been declining for many years due to: Habitat loss Accidental mortality, such as being killed by cars Pesticide use Poisoning of prairie dog colonies In some areas, the burrowing owl is considered endangered or threatened.
